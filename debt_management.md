## Financial Monitoring & Covenant Compliance

### Monthly Financial Review

**Key Metrics Dashboard**:
- Cash balance and reserve levels
- Monthly revenue vs. budget
- Expense control and variance analysis
- Debt service coverage ratio
- Membership growth and retention
- Program revenue performance

**Board Reporting**:
- Monthly financial statements
- Quarterly debt covenant compliance certificate
- Annual audit review
- Budget amendments if needed

### Covenant Compliance

**Typical CDFI Covenants**:

*Financial Covenants*:
- Maintain debt service coverage ratio ≥1.25x
- Maintain minimum unrestricted cash ≥$50,000
- Submit annual financial statements within 120 days of year-end
- No additional debt without lender approval

*Operational Covenants*:
- Maintain property insurance and liability coverage
- Pay property taxes and assessments timely
- Operate facility consistent with EDA requirements
- Notify lender of any material changes

**Compliance Monitoring**:
- Finance Committee reviews monthly
- Board approves compliance certification quarterly
- Executive Director responsible for timely reporting
- External accountant prepares annual audit

### Default Prevention

**Early Warning Signs**:
- Debt service coverage drops below 1.5x
- Operating reserve declines below $100,000
- Membership declines >10% from projections
- Revenue shortfall >15% of budget
- Unexpected major expense

**Proactive Response**:
- Immediate board notification
- Review budget and reduce expenses if needed
- Accelerate fundraising or member recruitment
- Communicate with lender early and often
- Develop recovery plan with specific actions

**Lender Communication**:
- Annual meeting to review performance
- Immediate notification of covenant violations or potential defaults
- Provide updated projections if circumstances change
- Request temporary relief or modification if needed

## Insurance & Risk Management

### Required Insurance Coverage

**Property Insurance**:
- Replacement cost coverage on buildings and improvements
- Named lender as loss payee
- Minimum coverage: Outstanding loan balance or replacement cost, whichever higher

**General Liability**:
- $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate minimum
- Covers member/visitor injuries and property damage
- Named lender as additional insured

**Equipment & Contents**:
- Coverage on tools, machinery, equipment
- Replacement cost basis
- Minimum: $150,000 - $200,000

**Directors & Officers Liability**:
- Protects board and staff from governance claims
- $1M coverage recommended
- Cyber liability rider for data breaches

**Workers Compensation**:
- Required by Arkansas law for employees
- Covers workplace injuries and illnesses

**Business Interruption** (Recommended):
- Covers lost revenue if facility damaged
- Helps maintain debt service during closure
- 6-12 month coverage period

**Annual Insurance Budget**: $15,000 (included in operating expenses)

## Exit Strategies & Contingency Planning

### If Unable to Sustain Debt Service

**Option 1: Sale to Mission-Aligned Buyer**
- Sell property to organization maintaining economic development use
- Satisfy EDA requirements
- Pay off CDFI loan
- Transition members and programs to new operator

**Option 2: Lease-Back Arrangement**
- Sell property but lease space for continued operations
- Reduces debt burden while maintaining programs
- Nonprofit renter vs. owner structure

**Option 3: Merger with Established Organization**
- Combine with larger nonprofit with financial stability
- Maintain Hub mission and programs
- Share overhead and governance

**Lender Protection**:
- First mortgage priority gives lender security
- Property value likely exceeds debt (federal investment)
- Mission-aligned CDFI may work with cooperative to restructure vs. foreclose

### Strategic Growth Beyond Year 1

**If Exceeding Projections**:
- Accelerate debt paydown to reduce interest costs
- Build larger operating reserve (9-12 months)
- Invest in facility improvements and equipment
- Expand programming and services
- Consider satellite locations or regional expansion

**Capital for Growth**:
- New Markets Tax Credit financing (if qualified)
- Historic tax credit financing (if building qualifies)
- State economic development incentives
- Large capital campaign for endowment
- Social impact investors for expansion
