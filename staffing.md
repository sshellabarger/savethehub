# Arkansas Innovation Hub Cooperative
## Staffing Plan & Human Resources

### Staffing Philosophy

The Hub employs a **lean core team supplemented by revenue-sharing educators** to maintain financial sustainability while delivering quality programming. This hybrid model reduces fixed overhead while incentivizing excellent instruction and member services.

**Core Principles**:
- Competitive compensation for full-time positions
- Revenue-sharing aligns educator incentives with program success
- Professional development and growth opportunities
- Cooperative culture and democratic workplace practices
- Diverse team reflecting Arkansas communities

## Revenue-Sharing Instructors (Contract)

### Structure

**Compensation Model**: 30% of gross class tuition revenue

**Example**: 
- Class with 9 students @ $65 = $585 gross
- Instructor receives: $175.50
- Hub receives: $409.50 (covers materials 10%, net 60%)

**Benefits of Model**:
- Variable cost structure: Instructors only paid when classes run
- Aligns instructor incentive with student satisfaction and enrollment
- Reduces Hub financial risk compared to fixed instructor salaries
- Enables diverse instructor pool without full-time commitment

### Instructor Categories

**Core Instructors** (4-8 individuals):
- Teach regularly (2-4 classes/month)
- Develop Hub curriculum and programming
- Annual income potential: $7,000 - $15,000
- Receive priority scheduling and support

**Occasional Instructors** (10-15 individuals):
- Teach specialty topics as scheduled
- Annual income potential: $2,000 - $6,000
- Expand program diversity and offerings

**Guest Instructors**:
- One-time or rare workshops
- Bring unique expertise
- Income per class: $150 - $250

### Instructor Support

**Hub Provides**:
- Curriculum development assistance
- Marketing and promotion of classes
- Registration and payment processing
- Materials purchasing and inventory
- Facility and equipment access
- Liability insurance coverage

**Instructor Requirements**:
- Signed independent contractor agreement
- Background check for youth programs
- Safety training and equipment certification
- Class plans and materials lists submitted in advance
- Professional conduct and attendance

## Hiring & Onboarding Process

### Executive Director Search

**Timeline**: 2-3 months

**Process**:
1. Board forms search committee
2. Develop position description and posting
3. Advertise nationally (Idealist, Chronicle of Philanthropy, Arkansas Nonprofit Alliance)
4. Application review and phone screens
5. Finalist interviews with search committee
6. Reference checks and background screening
7. Board approval and offer
8. 2-week onboarding before launch

**Key Selection Criteria**:
- Demonstrated leadership and management ability
- Financial acumen and fundraising experience
- Communication and relationship-building skills
- Alignment with cooperative values
- Cultural fit with Arkansas community

### Maker Educator Hiring

**Timeline**: 1 month per position

**Process**:
1. Executive Director develops job descriptions
2. Post to maker networks, vocational schools, technical colleges
3. Portfolio and teaching demonstration required
4. Skills assessment on equipment
5. Reference and background checks
6. 1-week equipment and curriculum training
7. Shadow experienced instructor for first class

**Key Selection Criteria**:
- Technical expertise demonstrated through portfolio
- Teaching ability shown in demonstration class
- Safety consciousness and professionalism
- Enthusiasm for maker education
- Customer service orientation

### Member Services Hiring

**Timeline**: 2-3 weeks

**Process**:
1. Post to Arkansas job boards and nonprofit networks
2. Resume and cover letter review
3. Phone screen for top candidates
4. In-person interview with scenario role-plays
5. Reference checks
6. 1-week orientation and training

**Key Selection Criteria**:
- Excellent interpersonal communication
- Organized and detail-oriented
- Marketing and social media competency
- Warm, welcoming personality
- Problem-solving ability

## Performance Management

### Annual Review Process

**Timeline**: Anniversary of hire date

**Components**:
1. Self-evaluation against annual goals
2. Supervisor evaluation and feedback
3. Peer feedback (360-degree for ED)
4. Performance discussion meeting
5. Goal-setting for coming year
6. Compensation review and adjustment

**Performance Metrics**:

*Executive Director*:
- Financial performance vs. budget
- Fundraising revenue achieved
- Member satisfaction scores
- Board and staff relations
- Strategic plan progress

*Maker Educators*:
- Class enrollment and fill rates
- Student satisfaction ratings
- Revenue generated through teaching and fabrication
- Equipment maintenance and safety record
- Member assistance quality

*Member Services*:
- Member recruitment and retention numbers
- Event attendance and engagement
- Social media and marketing metrics
- Member satisfaction feedback
- Administrative accuracy

### Corrective Action & Termination

**Progressive Discipline**:
1. Verbal warning with documentation
2. Written warning with improvement plan
3. Final written warning with timeline
4. Termination if no improvement

**Immediate Termination Reasons**:
- Safety violations endangering others
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats
- Illegal activity
- Gross misconduct

**Termination Process**:
- ED authority for non-ED staff (with board notification)
- Board authority for ED termination
- Exit interview conducted
- Final paycheck and benefits information
- Return of keys, badges, Hub property
- Knowledge transfer to remaining staff
