# Arkansas Innovation Hub Cooperative
## Marketing & Community Outreach Strategy

### Marketing Philosophy

The Hub's marketing approach emphasizes **community building over advertising**, focusing on authentic engagement, word-of-mouth growth, and demonstrable value. As a cooperative, every member is both a customer and an owner, making member satisfaction and advocacy the primary marketing engine.

### Brand Positioning

**Core Message**: "Your Community Makerspace. Your Voice. Your Impact."

**Value Propositions**:

*For Makers & Entrepreneurs*:
- Professional tools without capital investment
- Community of skilled peers and mentors
- Business development support and resources
- Ownership stake in community infrastructure

*For Families & Students*:
- Hands-on STEAM education
- Safe, supervised learning environment
- Creative skill development
- Affordable access to technology

*For Businesses*:
- Workforce development partnership
- Innovation and prototyping resources
- Community investment visibility
- Employee skill development opportunities

### Target Market Segmentation

#### Primary Audiences

**Makers & Artisans** (40% of voting members)
- Woodworkers, metalworkers, artists, craftspeople
- Age 25-65, middle income
- Value: 24/7 access to professional equipment
- Channels: Maker Faire, craft shows, local maker groups

**Entrepreneurs & Small Business** (25% of voting members)
- Product developers, inventors, small manufacturers
- Age 25-55, varied income
- Value: Prototyping resources and business support
- Channels: SCORE, Small Business Development Center, Chamber

**Tech Professionals** (20% of voting members)
- Software developers, engineers, tech workers
- Age 25-45, middle-to-upper income
- Value: Hands-on creation complement to digital work
- Channels: Tech meetups, Startup Arkansas, professional networks

**Hobbyists & Learners** (Supporting Members)
- Casual makers, DIY enthusiasts, lifelong learners
- Age 35-65, middle income
- Value: Affordable skill development and community
- Channels: Community education, senior centers, recreation departments

**Families** (Family Members)
- Parents seeking STEAM education for children
- Age 30-50, middle income
- Value: Educational enrichment and quality family time
- Channels: Schools, homeschool networks, parent groups

**Students** (Student Members)
- High school and college students
- Age 14-22, limited income
- Value: Career skill development and creative outlet
- Channels: Schools, youth organizations, universities

#### Secondary Audiences

**School Districts**
- K-12 administrators and teachers
- Value: Field trip destination, STEAM curriculum support
- Channels: Arkansas Association of Educational Administrators, direct outreach

**Homeschool Families**
- Arkansas LEARNS and EFA participants
- Value: Hands-on learning complement to home curriculum
- Channels: Homeschool cooperatives, Facebook groups, conventions

**Corporate Partners**
- HR and community relations departments
- Value: Employee benefit, community investment, innovation access
- Channels: Chamber of Commerce, direct business outreach

### Marketing Budget: $6,000/Year

**Allocation**:
- Digital marketing: $2,000 (33%)
- Print materials: $1,200 (20%)
- Event sponsorships: $1,500 (25%)
- Community partnerships: $800 (13%)
- Promotional items: $500 (9%)

**Philosophy**: Lean marketing budget emphasizing organic growth, member advocacy, and strategic partnerships over paid advertising.

### Launch Campaign (Months 1-3)

**Objectives**:
- Generate awareness of Hub reopening
- Recruit founding members (187 total by March)
- Build email list and social media following
- Establish community partnerships

**Tactics**:

*Pre-Launch (Month 1)*:
- Founding member recruitment: Early bird rates and special benefits
- Media outreach: Press releases to local news outlets
- Social media setup: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn pages
- Website launch: Information, membership signup, contact
- Email list building: Landing page with signup incentive

*Soft Launch (Month 2)*:
- Open house events: Weekly tours for prospective members
- Founder recognition: Public acknowledgment of early supporters
- Content marketing: Blog posts, video tours, member stories
- Community presentations: Service clubs, business groups, schools
- Partnership announcements: Corporate sponsors, grant partners

*Grand Opening (Month 3)*:
- Public celebration event with local officials
- Media coverage: TV, radio, newspaper features
- Maker demonstrations: Showcase equipment and capabilities
- Membership drive: Special signup incentives during event
- Community partnerships: Launch school district and homeschool programs
