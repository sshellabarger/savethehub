# Arkansas Innovation Hub Cooperative
## Governance Structure

### Cooperative Legal Framework

**Entity Type**: Arkansas Worker-Producer Cooperative (hybrid model)

**Incorporation**: Arkansas Nonprofit Corporation with Cooperative Provisions

**Tax Status**: 501(c)(3) public charity application planned

**Regulatory Compliance**:
- Arkansas Cooperative Association Act
- Federal Economic Development Administration (EDA) use requirements
- IRS nonprofit cooperative provisions

### Cooperative Principles

The Hub operates according to internationally recognized cooperative principles:

1. **Voluntary and Open Membership**: Open to all who can use services and accept member responsibilities
2. **Democratic Member Control**: One-member, one-vote on major decisions
3. **Member Economic Participation**: Members contribute equitably and control capital
4. **Autonomy and Independence**: Member-controlled, self-governing organization
5. **Education, Training, and Information**: Support member and community education
6. **Cooperation Among Cooperatives**: Work with local and regional cooperative networks
7. **Concern for Community**: Sustainable development of the North Little Rock community

### Membership Structure

#### Voting Members
- **Monthly Fee**: $80
- **Governance Rights**: Full voting rights on board elections and major decisions
- **Access**: 24/7 facility access, all equipment and tools
- **Target**: Serious makers, entrepreneurs, and regular users
- **Year 1 Goal**: 65 voting members

#### Supporting Members
- **Monthly Fee**: $50
- **Governance Rights**: Advisory participation, no voting rights
- **Access**: Scheduled facility access, basic equipment
- **Target**: Casual makers, community supporters
- **Year 1 Goal**: 160 supporting members

#### Family Members
- **Monthly Fee**: $80
- **Governance Rights**: One household vote
- **Access**: Family access for up to 4 household members
- **Target**: Families with children interested in maker education
- **Year 1 Goal**: 26 family memberships

#### Student Members
- **Monthly Fee**: $25
- **Governance Rights**: Limited voting rights on student-related matters
- **Access**: Supervised facility access, educational equipment
- **Target**: High school and college students
- **Year 1 Goal**: 22 student members

#### Corporate Members
- **Monthly Fee**: $400
- **Governance Rights**: Advisory participation, designated employee access
- **Access**: Corporate team building, innovation space usage
- **Target**: Local businesses supporting workforce development
- **Year 1 Goal**: 8 corporate members

### Board of Directors

#### Composition
- **Size**: 7-9 directors
- **Member-Elected**: Minimum 5 voting member representatives
- **Appointed Seats**: Up to 2 community stakeholder representatives
- **Ex-Officio**: Executive Director (non-voting)

#### Election Process
- Annual elections at membership meeting
- Staggered 3-year terms
- Voting members elect member directors
- Board nominates community stakeholder directors for member approval

#### Board Responsibilities
- Strategic planning and policy direction
- Financial oversight and budget approval
- Executive Director hiring, evaluation, and compensation
- Major capital expenditure approval (>$10,000)
- Property acquisition and debt authorization
- Bylaw amendments and governance policies
- Grant application review and acceptance
- Annual reporting to membership

#### Board Committees

**Finance Committee**
- Monthly financial review
- Budget development
- Audit oversight
- Investment policy
- Debt management

**Governance Committee**
- Board recruitment and development
- Bylaw review and recommendations
- Conflict of interest policies
- Board self-evaluation

**Program Committee**
- Educational program review
- Equipment acquisition recommendations
- Safety policy oversight
- Member services evaluation

**Development Committee**
- Fundraising strategy
- Foundation and corporate relations
- Community partnership development
- Member recruitment campaigns

### Management Structure

#### Executive Director
**Reports to**: Board of Directors

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Day-to-day operational management
- Staff supervision and development
- Financial management and reporting
- Grant management and compliance
- Community and stakeholder relations
- Strategic plan implementation
- Board support and communication

**Authority**:
- Operational decisions within approved budget
- Staff hiring and supervision
- Vendor contracts <$10,000
- Program development and scheduling
- Member services and policies

#### Maker Educators (4 positions)
**Reports to**: Executive Director

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Class instruction and curriculum development
- Fabrication service delivery
- Equipment maintenance and safety
- Member training and support
- Program revenue generation through 30% revenue share

**Revenue Sharing Model**:
- 30% of class tuition revenue
- 20% of fabrication service revenue
- Aligns compensation with program success
- Reduces fixed overhead costs

#### Member Services & Outreach Coordinator
**Reports to**: Executive Director

**Key Responsibilities**:
- Member recruitment and onboarding
- Community outreach and marketing
- Event coordination
- Volunteer management
- Social media and communications

### Decision-Making Authority

#### Board Authority (Requires Board Vote)
- Annual budget approval
- Executive Director hiring/termination
- Property acquisition or major leases
- Debt authorization >$50,000
- Bylaw amendments
- Membership policy changes
- Strategic plan approval

#### Membership Authority (Requires Member Vote)
- Board of Directors elections
- Fundamental cooperative structure changes
- Dissolution or merger decisions
- Significant mission changes
- Large capital campaigns (>$500,000)

#### Executive Director Authority
- Operational budget execution
- Staff management within approved headcount
- Vendor contracts <$10,000
- Program development
- Routine facility management
- Grant application submission (with board notification)

### Stakeholder Advisory Groups

#### Education Advisory Council
**Purpose**: Guide educational programming and K-12 partnerships

**Composition**:
- Arkansas school district representatives
- Homeschool organization leaders
- Higher education partners
- STEAM education experts

**Meeting Frequency**: Quarterly

#### Maker Community Council
**Purpose**: Advise on equipment, tools, and maker services

**Composition**:
- Experienced maker members
- Local artisans and craftspeople
- Small business owners using fabrication services
- Technical experts in various disciplines

**Meeting Frequency**: Monthly

#### Economic Development Partners
**Purpose**: Ensure alignment with regional economic development goals

**Composition**:
- North Little Rock Chamber of Commerce
- Arkansas Economic Development Commission
- Small Business Administration representatives
- EDA regional office (advisory)

**Meeting Frequency**: Bi-annual

### Conflict of Interest Policy

**Disclosure Requirements**:
- Annual disclosure statements from all board members
- Disclosure of any potential conflicts before relevant decisions
- Recusal from votes involving personal or business interests

**Prohibited Transactions**:
- Self-dealing in property or service contracts
- Loans to board members or staff
- Compensation for board service (except expense reimbursement)
- Personal use of cooperative assets for private gain

### Transparency & Accountability

**Public Documents**:
- Annual financial statements
- IRS Form 990 (when applicable)
- Board meeting minutes (executive session excluded)
- Bylaws and governance policies
- Strategic plans and annual reports

**Member Communications**:
- Monthly operational updates
- Quarterly financial summaries
- Annual membership meeting
- Regular program and event calendars

**External Reporting**:
- EDA annual economic impact reporting
- Grant funder required reports
- Arkansas Secretary of State annual filings
- IRS nonprofit compliance (when 501(c)(3) granted)

### Amendment Procedures

**Bylaw Amendments**:
- Board proposal with 2/3 vote
- Member ratification at annual or special meeting
- 30-day notice to membership
- Simple majority of voting members required

**Policy Amendments**:
- Board authority for operational policies
- Member notification within 30 days
- Member petition process for policy review

### Succession Planning

**Executive Director Transition**:
- Board designates interim leadership
- National search process for permanent replacement
- 3-month overlap period if possible
- Knowledge transfer documentation required

**Board Continuity**:
- Staggered terms prevent complete turnover
- Vice Chair succession to Chair role
- New board member orientation program
- Institutional knowledge documentation

### Legal & Compliance Structure

**Legal Counsel**: Retained for corporate governance, contracts, and compliance

**Accounting Services**: Monthly bookkeeping and annual audit preparation

**Insurance Coverage**:
- Directors & Officers liability insurance
- General liability coverage
- Property and equipment insurance
- Workers' compensation insurance

**Regulatory Compliance**:
- Annual Arkansas cooperative filings
- EDA economic development reporting
- Grant compliance requirements
- Employment law compliance
- Safety and building code compliance
