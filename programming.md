# Arkansas Innovation Hub Cooperative
## Educational Programming & Services

### Programming Philosophy

The Hub delivers **hands-on, project-based learning experiences** that develop technical skills, creative confidence, and entrepreneurial mindset. Programming serves dual purposes: community impact through education and earned income through sustainable pricing models that value both learner and instructor.

### Program Categories Overview

**Adult Education**: Classes and workshops for skill development and hobby exploration
**Youth Programs**: K-12 STEAM education, summer camps, after-school activities
**School Partnerships**: Field trips and curriculum support for Arkansas school districts
**Fabrication Services**: Custom project work and production support
**Grant-Funded Programs**: Workforce development and specialized initiatives
**Community Events**: Open houses, maker faires, competitions, and celebrations

## Adult Education Programs

### Class Structure & Economics

**Revenue Model**:
- Student tuition: 60% to Hub
- Instructor compensation: 30% to instructor
- Materials cost: 10% of tuition

**Example Class Economics** (9 students @ $65):
- Gross revenue: $585
- Instructor share: $175.50
- Materials: $58.50
- Net to Hub: $351

This model incentivizes instructor excellence while maintaining Hub financial sustainability.

### Class Types

#### Weekend Workshops (3-4 hours)
**Schedule**: Saturdays and Sundays, 10am-2pm or 1pm-5pm
**Class Size**: 6-12 students
**Pricing**: $65-85 per student
**Frequency**: 20 classes/month (April-November)

**Sample Offerings**:
- *Introduction to Woodworking*: Basic joinery, tool safety, build a cutting board
- *Laser Cutting Fundamentals*: Vector design, material selection, create custom art
- *3D Printing Basics*: CAD design intro, printer operation, print take-home project
- *Metalworking Essentials*: Welding basics, metal forming, create wall art
- *Sewing & Textiles*: Industrial sewing machines, pattern reading, make a tote bag
- *Electronics & Arduino*: Circuits, programming, build a smart home device

#### Weeknight Classes (2-3 hours)
**Schedule**: Tuesday-Thursday, 6pm-9pm
**Class Size**: 8-15 students
**Pricing**: $45-65 per student
**Frequency**: 8 classes/month

**Sample Offerings**:
- *Power Tools for Beginners*: Safety certification and hands-on practice
- *Digital Design for Makers*: Inkscape, Fusion 360, design for fabrication
- *Small Business for Makers*: Pricing, marketing, selling handmade goods
- *CNC Router Workshop*: CAM software, toolpath creation, carving project
- *Leatherworking*: Hand tools, stitching, create a leather wallet
- *Screen Printing*: Emulsion process, multi-color printing, design t-shirt

#### Intensive Courses (2-3 sessions, multi-week)
**Schedule**: Consecutive weeks, 3-4 hours per session
**Class Size**: 6-10 students
**Pricing**: $150-250 total
**Frequency**: 2 intensives/month

**Sample Offerings**:
- *Furniture Making Series* (3 weeks): Design and build a side table or bench
- *Advanced Electronics* (3 weeks): PCB design, surface mount soldering, finished device
- *CNC Certification* (2 weeks): Safe operation, programming, independent project
- *Entrepreneurship Bootcamp* (3 weeks): Business planning, prototyping, pitch preparation
- *Metal Sculpture* (3 weeks): Welding, forming, finishing, create wall or floor sculpture

### Instructor Recruitment & Development

**Instructor Qualifications**:
- Demonstrated expertise in subject matter
- Teaching experience or willingness to train
- Liability insurance or covered under Hub policy
- Background check clearance for youth programs
- Safety certification for equipment instruction

**Instructor Onboarding**:
- Hub facilities orientation and safety training
- Teaching methodology workshop
- Curriculum development support
- First class co-teaching with experienced instructor
- Ongoing professional development opportunities

**Instructor Community**:
- Monthly instructor meetings for curriculum sharing

- Recognition and appreciation events
- Revenue sharing creates instructor investment in quality

**Instructor Pipeline**:
- Advanced members transition to teaching roles
- Partnership with UALR and community colleges
- Retired professionals seeking part-time teaching
- Local artists and makers as subject matter experts

**Age Groups**: Elementary (K-5), Middle School (6-8), High School (9-12)
**Session Length**: 8-week sessions, 3 sessions per school year
**Pricing**: $200-250 per 8-week session

**Middle School Programs** (Ages 11-14):

**High School Programs** (Ages 14-18):

### Saturday Workshops for Youth

**Schedule**: Saturdays, 10am-12pm or 1pm-3pm

### Summer Camps

**Schedule**: June-August, 5-day camps (Mon-Fri)
**Sessions**: 9am-3pm daily
**Age Groups**: 7-10, 11-14, 15-17
**Pricing**: $250-350 per week
**Capacity**: 20 students per camp

**Camp Themes**:
- *Maker Camp*: Different project each day, build and take home
- *Robotics Camp*: Build robots, program challenges, competition
- *Digital Design Camp*: 3D printing, laser cutting, design software
- *Entrepreneurship Camp*: Invent product, prototype, pitch to parents

**Camp Economic Model**:
- 8 camps over summer (2 per week for 4 weeks)
- Average 18 students per camp @ $300
- Gross revenue per camp: $5,400
- Counselor costs (2 @ $500/week): $1,000
- Materials: $500
- Net per camp: $3,900
- Summer camp revenue: ~$31,000

**Pricing**: $250-350 for 2-hour party (up to 12 kids)

**Party Packages**:

**Party Revenue**:

## Fabrication Services

### Custom Project Services

**Service Types**:
- Laser cutting and engraving
- 3D printing (PLA, PETG, specialty materials)
- CNC routing (wood, plastic, aluminum)
- Vinyl cutting and graphics
- Small-batch production runs

**Pricing Model**:
- Setup fee: $25-50 per project
- Hourly machine time: $30-60/hour depending on equipment
- Materials: Cost + 25% markup
- Design services: $50/hour
- Rush fees: 50% premium for 24-hour turnaround

**Target Markets**:
- Small businesses needing prototypes
- Artists creating gallery pieces
- Hobbyists with complex projects
- Event planners (signage, decorations)
- Architects and designers (models)

**Service Economics**:
- Target: $2,000/month average
- Annual fabrication revenue: ~$24,000
- Operator time (20%): $4,800
- Materials and overhead: $7,200
- Net revenue: $12,000

### Member Discount Structure

**Member Pricing**:
- Voting members: 30% discount on services
- Supporting members: 20% discount
- Student members: 25% discount
- Family members: 25% discount

**Strategy**: Member discounts encourage utilization and satisfaction while still generating revenue from commercial accounts.
